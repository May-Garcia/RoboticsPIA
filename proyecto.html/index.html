The system selected for this project is the muscular system. The
muscular system is a network of specialized tissues, providing the
strength and mobility necessary for various functions of the human
body. Composed of more than 600 muscles, this system is responsible
for bodily activities. Robotics will be worked together with biology
to create a robot that can solve a problem in the muscular system, a
robot with fast and efficient medical assistance.

Research Data

Functions and Importance

Movement: Muscles generate force to perform voluntary and involuntary movements
such as walking and running and even breathing and blinking, the muscular
system allows interaction with the environment and the internal functioning of the body.
Support: Muscles stabilize the body and allow it to mantain posture during rest and movement
Heat production: Heat is generated as a metabolic byproduct. Maintains a constant body temperature,
especially in cold weather or during exercise.
Circulartion and Internal Functions: The cardiac muscle pumps blood constantly, ensuring oxygenation of the tissues.
Smooth muscles of internal organs, such as the intestines, enable essential
processes such as bladder emptying. Protection: Muscles act as a barrier that cushions shocks and protects vital organs.
They also contribute to the stabilization of joints, reducing the risk of injury.

Organs and their functions

Skeletal Muscles: These are the muscles that allow voluntary movements. They are attached to the bones and
are responsible for actions such as walking, running, and lifting objects.
Cardiac Muscle: This is the muscle of the heart. It is responsible for pumping blood throughout the body.
The cardiac muscle is involuntary and works continuously without conscious control.
Smooth Muscles: These muscles are found in the walls of internal organs, such as the intestines and blood vessels.
They are responsible for involuntary movements, such as digestion and blood circulation.

For more information please visit here: Cleveland Clinic

Health Problems

Cramp: is an involuntary, intense and painful contraction of several groups of muscle fibers
due to an exaggerated effort on these fibers. It usually lasts a short time; it may be
because the muscle is not well warmed up and dehydration
Contracture: It is a muscular discomfort that is accompanied by pain on palpation over the damaged
muscle and difficulty in its movement. It appears suddenly and may last several days.
Strain: It is an exaggerated stretching of the muscle without it breaking; it is the
well-known like pull. It occurs in muscles taken to the limit of their elasticity.
Concussion: It occurs when the muscle is hit or is hit against a rigid structure.
They are very frequent in contact sports. It produces diffuse pain and inflammation.
Muscle rupture: occurs immediately, and the skin becomes red and hot. The pain is intense, and the
sinking of the affected area can be appreciated. Surgery is required.

Solutions:

Cramp: To relieve cramps, start with simple remedies like stretching the affected muscle, applying
heat or cold, and taking over-the-counter pain relievers
Contracture: To treat contractures, rest the affected muscle, apply heat or cold, and take anti-inflammatory
medications. Gentle stretching and physical therapy may also help.
Strain: For strains, rest the affected muscle, apply ice to reduce swelling, and take pain relievers.
Gradual stretching and strengthening exercises can aid recovery.
Concussion: For muscle concussions, rest the affected area, apply ice to reduce swelling, and take
pain relievers. Gentle stretching and physical therapy may be necessary for recovery.
Muscle rupture: For muscle ruptures, immediate medical attention is crucial. Treatment may involve
immobilization, physical therapy, and in severe cases, surgery.

Robotic solution

What our prototype will do to help with these problems is that, when our robot
detects that a person has suffered a cramp or some other problem in their muscles,
it will reach the affected area and apply cooling spray and then begin to give a small
massage to relax the area.

Both sticks protruding from the robot are in charge of massaging the affected area.
The rear part is the one that will release the cooling spray on the area.
The base was designed so that it can rotate without problems.
